## Overview
Standardized microgrid solutions often fail in scenarios with unique policy constraints or unusual load profiles. A differentiated, detail‑oriented approach builds a defensible market position.

## Steps
1. **Deeply research local regulations** for each project – go beyond general policy to identify specific constraints (export limits, penalty clauses, tariff nuances).
2. **Embed policy constraints into control algorithms** – modify the optimization engine to treat local rules as hard boundaries.
3. **Perform advanced load analysis** – not just power/energy, but also working principles (see `perform-detailed-load-analysis`).
4. **Build and maintain a library of special load models** – systematically document every unusual load type encountered.
5. **Test control strategies against real loads**, not only standard test benches.
6. **Iterate project by project** – continuously enrich the experience database, enabling increasingly accurate forecasts for future projects.
7. **Communicate this differentiation** – demonstrate to clients how fine‑grained attention prevents costly post‑construction corrections.

## Decision Points
- If a potential project has highly unique operational details that mass‑market vendors ignore: **pursue it as a high‑value niche** rather than competing on price.

## Expected Result
A reputation for solving the hardest microgrid challenges that generalist providers avoid, leading to long‑term partnerships with large energy companies and premium pricing.
